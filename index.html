1
Find the right machine
Look for green machines labeled みどりの窓口券売機 or blue JR multi-function machines with a large touchscreen. Orange IC card machines only work for local trains.
Press [English] in the top-right corner when the machine starts up.
2
Select "Shinkansen" on the main menu
Tap "Shinkansen" or "Express / Limited Express Tickets" from the home screen.

3
Enter your destination
Type in romaji: TOKYO, KYOTO, HIROSHIMA, HAKATA, etc. For Osaka, type SHIN-OSAKA — that's where Shinkansen stops, not regular Osaka station.
4
Choose your train & departure time
A list sorted by departure time appears showing train name, departure, and arrival. Tap your preferred train.
⚠ Nozomi and Mizuho are NOT covered by JR Pass. Select Hikari or Sakura instead if using JR Pass.
5
Choose seat type
You'll see three options:
Select seat type
自由席 · Unreserved
指定席 · Reserved
グリーン車 · Green Car
For first-timers: pick 指定席 (reserved). You get a guaranteed assigned seat.
6
Pick your seat on the map
White = available, gray = taken. A/E are window seats. D-E side often faces Mt. Fuji (Tokyo↔Nagoya).
7
Pay
Cash (¥1000, ¥5000, ¥10000), credit cards (chip first), or Suica IC card. If card fails, try the staffed window: みどりの窓口.
8
Take BOTH tickets
Two tickets come out: 乗車券 (base fare) and 特急券 (Shinkansen supplement). You need both at the gate — don't lose either one.
✓ Done! Now use the "Gates & platforms" tab to board.

自
自由席 · Unreserved (cheaper)
Sit anywhere in designated cars (usually 1–3 or 13–16). No seat guarantee. A few hundred yen cheaper.
⚠ On holidays and Golden Week, these cars fill completely. You may stand for hours.
指
指定席 · Reserved (recommended)
Your specific car + row + seat is assigned. Your ticket shows e.g. 7号車 12D = Car 7, Seat 12D. A few hundred yen more than unreserved.
Seat layout (2+3 rows):
A = window left · B = middle left · C = aisle left
D = aisle right · E = window right
グ
グリーン車 · Green Car (first class)
Wider seats (2+2), more recline, quieter. Significantly more expensive. Great for long overnight-ish journeys.
1
Find the Shinkansen entrance
Look for 新幹線 signs (bullet train icon). Separate from local train gates. Don't use local train gates — your ticket won't work there.
Signs in English at all major stations. Follow the bullet train icon 🚄
2
Insert BOTH tickets at the gate
Shinkansen gates have special dual-slot readers. Insert the 乗車券 and 特急券 together (or one right after the other). Both are returned — pick them up on the other side.
⚠ Don't forget to take your tickets out the other end of the gate!
3
Find your platform (NOT on your ticket!)
Platform number is not printed on your ticket. Check the departure boards inside the Shinkansen concourse. Find your train name + departure time.
Trainのぞみ 15 · Nozomi 15

4
Find your car on the platform
Look for your car number (号車) painted on the ground markers. Stand in that queue. Trains stop at the exact same spot every time.
If your ticket says 7号車, look for the "7" marker on the platform and queue there.
5
Board and find your seat
Seat numbers are on overhead displays inside the car. Row numbers increase from the front. A = window left, E = window right.
✓ Shinkansen is extremely punctual. Board at least 3 minutes before departure.
